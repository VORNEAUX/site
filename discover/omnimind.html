The prompt problem

AI is powerful. Most people just don't know what to type.

Type something vague and you get something vague back — and it's almost never the model's fault.

"Help me write a resume."

Generic advice, surface-level suggestions — the kind of answer that sounds plausible but doesn't move anything forward.

"Give me marketing ideas."

The difference between a mediocre response and a genuinely useful one almost always comes down to the prompt itself.

"Why isn't this working for me?"

People who consistently get great results from AI have simply learned how to frame the request. That's a learnable skill — or a shortcut you can buy.
